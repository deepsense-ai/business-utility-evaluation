1. **Time & Batch Linking**:
   - Maintenance events always occur in the early morning (completed by 05:30), while production batches on the same day start later (from 06:00 onwards).
   - Thus, each batch was uniquely mapped to its most recent preceding maintenance event on the same machine (where the maintenance day $D_{maint} \le D_{batch}$).
2. **Isolating Maintenance-Caused Faults vs. Natural Variation**:
   - Within any maintenance interval, we analyzed the rejection rate per product. 
   - Maintenance-induced "hidden faults" are persistent and affect all batches of that product in that interval (yielding a $100\%$ rejection rate). 
   - Fractional rejection rates (where some batches of the same product in the same interval are accepted) represent natural variation and background defects, which were ruled out.
