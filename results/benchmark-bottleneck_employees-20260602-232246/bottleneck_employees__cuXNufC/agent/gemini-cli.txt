### Proposed Strategy for Identifying Bottleneck Employees

To identify which employees are delaying work due to overload or poor performance from the CSV dataset, we can employ one of two strategies:

#### Approach 1: Metric-Based Outlier Detection (Within-Role Statistical Analysis)
*   **Mechanism:** Define performance metrics per employee:
    *   **Code Review Turnaround:** Mean and median duration of `completed_at - requested_at` for code reviews.
    *   **Approval Turnaround:** Mean and median duration of `resolved_at - requested_at` for approvals.
    *   **Task Completion Turnaround:** Average active duration of assigned tasks (`completed_at - assigned_at`).
    *   **Dependency Blocking Impact:** Total duration and number of times their tasks blocked others in `task_dependency_001.csv`.
    *   **Overload Indicators:** Total meeting hours (from `meeting_001.csv` & participant lists) and message volume.
*   **Analysis:** Group employees by `role` and `seniority_level` to calculate Z-scores or IQR outliers within each peer group. Anyone with a Z-score of $+2.5$ or greater in turnaround delays or dependency blocks is flagged as a bottleneck.
*   **Pros:** Highly objective, accounts for different role expectations, and mathematically isolates extreme underperformers or overloaded staff.

#### Approach 2: Pipeline Delay Contribution Model (Lifecycle Critical-Path Analysis)
*   **Mechanism:** Construct a task lifecycle flow for every task (Creation $\rightarrow$ Assignment $\rightarrow$ Review $\rightarrow$ QA Testing $\rightarrow$ Approval $\rightarrow$ Deployment). For each task, compute the duration of each phase. Measure the delay of each phase compared to the company average. Attribute the delay to the employee responsible for that phase.
*   **Analysis:** Calculate the cumulative delay contribution of each employee. Rank employees by their total delay impact on the delivery pipeline.
*   **Pros:** Directly measures the business impact/ripple effect of delays and identifies people whose slow delivery blocks subsequent downstream work.

---

### Recommendation
We recommend **Approach 1** (Statistical Outlier Detection within peer groups), enhanced by incorporating the **Dependency Blocking Impact** from Approach 2 as one of its key dimensions. This ensures that we don't unfairly penalize juniors compared to seniors, while precisely identifying overloaded or underperforming individuals who are statistically distinct from their peers.
