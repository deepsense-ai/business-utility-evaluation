### Methodology & Modeling
1. **Target Cities:** Initial inspection showed a sharp division in city sizes—exactly 30 cities have a non-negligible user base (ranging from 98 to 1,512 users), while the remaining 50 cities have exactly 1 user each. Analysis was restricted to the 30 active cities.

Their behavior differs significantly; for example, regular users have a purchase-to-interest conversion rate of ~28%, verified users ~18%, and business users ~1.5%. Modeling demand required adjusting for the user type distribution of each city.
3. **Statistical Models:**
   - **Demographic Model:** Calculated expected purchases per city-category based on the city's user-type demographics and global category purchases per user-type.
   - **Interest Model:** Calculated expected purchases based on local category-specific interest (clicks, searches, favorites) weighted by the global conversion rates of each user-type.
4. **Anomalies Detection:** Applied Poisson probability tests to find city-category pairs where actual purchase counts are statistically significantly lower than expected under **both** models ($p < 0.05$ under both demographic and interest expectations, and actual purchases $< 60\%$ of expected purchases).
