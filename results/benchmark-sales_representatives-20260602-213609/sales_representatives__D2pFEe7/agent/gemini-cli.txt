### Methodology
1. **Mapping Representatives to Stores:** By analyzing `store_visit_*.csv` files, we discovered that each store is managed by a primary representative who conducts the vast majority (~85%) of visits. Each representative manages exactly 5 stores, and every region contains exactly 5 representatives and 25 stores.

3. **Statistical Modeling:** We fit a high-resolution, multi-level ordinary least squares (OLS) linear regression model of daily/monthly sales (at the store-product level) on all external, environmental confounders:
   - **Store Level:** Store size (`store_size_sqm`), traffic (`customer_count`), type (`store_type`), location (`location`), and region (`region`).
   - **Product Level:** Brand, category, and unit price.
   - **Market Factors:** Competitor brand facings and promotions.
   - **Temporal and Promotional Factors:** Seasonal month controls, promo active days, and discounted prices.
4. **Outlier Detection:** From the regression residuals and representative-specific dummy coefficients under both units sold and revenue models, we calculated standard Z-scores. Representatives with **Z-scores < -1.75** (highly statistically significant negative outliers) were flagged as underperforming.
