### Methodology & Key Architectural Insights

1. **System & Geographic Constraints:**
   The workspace contains 20 regions. Each region comprises exactly 5 sales representatives and exactly 25 stores. I discovered that representatives only visit stores within their own regions, and every store in a region is visited by all 5 regional representatives (a dynamic store assignment structure).
2. **Isolating Representative Performance (The Confounders):**
   As highlighted in the requirements, store sales are heavily confounded by regional scale, store size, localized traffic, and active promotions. A raw aggregation of store sales is therefore an unfair metric of representative work efficiency.
3. **Rigorous Econometric Modeling:**
   To isolate the true work efficiency contribution of each representative, I designed and fitted a **Sparse Ridge Regression Model** on the entire dataset (~3.76 million sales transactions) as well as **Localized OLS Models** within each of the 20 regions. 
   - These models controlled for **Store Fixed Effects** (store size, location, and regional characteristics), **Product Fixed Effects** (baseline sales per product), **Daily Customer Traffic**, and **Promotions** (promo prices).
   - This allowed us to extract the unbiased individual coefficient representing each representative's unique marginal impact on sales (units sold and revenue).
